================================================================================
1. PURPOSE
================================================================================

This policy establishes the minimum retention periods for BMO data assets, the
approved methods for secure data disposal, and the responsibilities of data
custodians in managing data throughout its lifecycle.

Compliance with this policy is mandatory for all BMO employees, contractors,
and third-party service providers who create, store, process, or dispose of BMO
data.

================================================================================
2. SCOPE
================================================================================

This policy applies to all BMO data regardless of format (structured, semi-
structured, unstructured) and storage medium (on-premises servers, cloud
storage, endpoint devices, removable media, paper records).

================================================================================
3. REGULATORY BASIS
================================================================================

Retention periods defined in this policy reflect the minimum requirements under:

  - Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions (OSFI) Guideline E-21
  - Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA)
  - Financial Transactions and Reports Analysis Centre of Canada (FINTRAC)
    Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Act
  - Ontario Electronic Commerce Act, 2000
  - BMO Group Internal Audit requirements

Where applicable law requires a longer retention period than specified in this
policy, the legal requirement takes precedence.

================================================================================
4. RETENTION SCHEDULE
================================================================================

4.1 CUSTOMER RECORDS

  Customer account records (KYC, account opening, agreements)
    Retention: 7 years after account closure
    Disposal: Secure shredding (paper); cryptographic erasure (digital)

  Transaction records (payments, transfers, trade confirmations)
    Retention: 7 years from transaction date
    Disposal: Secure shredding (paper); cryptographic erasure (digital)

  Customer complaints and dispute records
    Retention: 5 years from resolution date
    Disposal: Secure shredding (paper); cryptographic erasure (digital)

4.2 EMPLOYEE RECORDS

  Payroll and compensation records
    Retention: 7 years after employment termination
    Disposal: Secure shredding (paper); cryptographic erasure (digital)

  Performance reviews and disciplinary records
    Retention: 5 years after employment termination
    Disposal: Secure shredding (paper); cryptographic erasure (digital)

  Background check and reference records
    Retention: Duration of employment + 2 years
    Disposal: Secure shredding; access-controlled deletion

4.3 AUDIT AND COMPLIANCE RECORDS

  Internal audit reports and working papers
    Retention: 10 years
    Disposal: Cryptographic erasure; certificate of destruction required

  Regulatory examination records and correspondence
    Retention: 10 years from examination close date
    Disposal: Cryptographic erasure; certificate of destruction required

  AML/ATF suspicious transaction reports (STRs)
    Retention: 5 years from date of filing
    Disposal: Cryptographic erasure; FINTRAC notification required before disposal

4.4 TECHNOLOGY AND SYSTEM RECORDS

  System access logs and authentication records
    Retention: 2 years (OSFI E-21 minimum)
    Disposal: Secure deletion; log integrity hash retained for 5 years

  Network device configuration backups
    Retention: Current version + 3 prior versions; minimum 1 year
    Disposal: Cryptographic erasure from configuration management system

  Security incident records and forensic images
    Retention: 5 years from incident closure
    Disposal: Cryptographic erasure; Legal hold must be confirmed as lifted

4.5 MARKETING AND COMMUNICATIONS RECORDS

  Marketing campaign records and customer consent logs
    Retention: 3 years from last customer interaction
    Disposal: Cryptographic erasure; CASL compliance log retained separately

  Internal communications (email, instant messaging)
    Retention: 3 years general; 7 years if related to a regulatory matter
    Disposal: Automated purge via email lifecycle management system

================================================================================
5. DATA DISPOSAL PROCEDURES
================================================================================

5.1 DIGITAL MEDIA DISPOSAL

All digital media containing BMO data must be disposed of using one of the
following NIST SP 800-88 compliant methods:

  a) Cryptographic Erasure
     Preferred method for cloud storage, SSDs, and encrypted volumes.
     Destroy the encryption key using the approved key management system (KMS).
     A certificate of key destruction must be retained for 3 years.

  b) Secure Overwrite
     For mechanical hard drives (HDDs) not using full-disk encryption.
     Apply a minimum of 3-pass DoD 5220.22-M overwrite using approved tooling.
     Overwrite verification log must be retained.

  c) Physical Destruction
     For damaged media or media containing Tier 1 (Highly Confidential) data.
     Degauss and shred using a cross-cut shredder certified to DIN 66399 Level P-5.
     Use an approved third-party vendor. Obtain and retain a certificate of
     destruction referencing media serial numbers.

5.2 PAPER RECORD DISPOSAL

All paper records containing personal or confidential information must be
disposed of in locked shredding bins only. BMO-approved vendor Iron Mountain
collects and cross-cut shreds all paper waste under a chain-of-custody agreement.

Do not place confidential paper records in general recycling bins.

5.3 REMOVABLE MEDIA (USB, OPTICAL DISCS, TAPES)

  - USB drives: Physical destruction only (approved shredder on each floor)
  - Backup tapes: Degauss + physical destruction by approved vendor
  - Optical discs: Shred using cross-cut optical disc shredder

================================================================================
6. DATA HOLDS (LITIGATION AND REGULATORY)
================================================================================

When BMO receives a litigation hold notice or regulatory preservation order,
normal retention schedules are suspended for affected data. Data custodians must:

  1. Immediately notify the Information Governance team: data-governance@bmo.com
  2. Tag affected data repositories with the hold reference number in the
     Data Asset Inventory (DAI) system
  3. Do NOT delete or modify any data under hold, even if past its scheduled
     retention date
  4. Await written confirmation from Legal before resuming normal disposal

================================================================================
7. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
================================================================================

  Data Owner:         Accountable for defining retention requirements for their
                      data domain; approves exceptions to retention schedule.

  Data Custodian:     Responsible for implementing retention controls in systems
                      and executing disposal according to this policy.

  Information Governance Team: Maintains retention schedule; reviews exceptions;
                      conducts annual compliance audits.

  All Employees:      Must not retain personal or confidential data beyond
                      policy limits on personal devices or unauthorised storage.

================================================================================
8. EXCEPTIONS
================================================================================

Requests to deviate from retention periods defined in this policy require:

  - Written justification from the Data Owner
  - Approval from the Chief Data Officer or delegate
  - Documentation in the BMO Exception Register (ServiceNow: DRTPOL-EXC)

Unapproved retention of data beyond policy limits is a violation of BMO's Code
of Conduct and may result in disciplinary action.

================================================================================
9. POLICY VIOLATIONS
================================================================================

Premature deletion of records subject to a legal or regulatory hold is a serious
compliance breach. Report suspected violations immediately to:

  - Information Governance: data-governance@bmo.com
  - Compliance Hotline: 1-888-BMO-COMP (anonymous reporting available)
