================================================================================
1. PURPOSE
================================================================================

This policy defines BMO's approach to detecting, containing, eradicating, and
recovering from cyber security incidents. It establishes roles and escalation
paths to minimise the impact of incidents on BMO's customers, operations, and
reputation.

All employees are required to report suspected security incidents immediately.
Failure to report is itself a policy violation.

================================================================================
2. SCOPE
================================================================================

This policy applies to all BMO information systems, networks, cloud environments,
third-party-managed services, and endpoints accessing BMO resources, regardless
of geography.

================================================================================
3. INCIDENT CLASSIFICATION
================================================================================

BMO classifies security incidents by severity:

  P1 — CRITICAL
  Definition: Active breach with confirmed data exfiltration or ransomware
              encryption; systemic failure of a Tier 1 application; incidents
              meeting OSFI Technology and Cyber Risk Guideline reporting thresholds.
  Response SLA: Incident Commander engaged within 15 minutes.
  Notification: CISO, CEO, Board Risk Committee, OSFI (within 24 hours if
                material); Canadian Centre for Cyber Security (CCCS).

  P2 — HIGH
  Definition: Suspected breach under active investigation; partial compromise of
              a Tier 1 system; successful phishing attack with credential theft.
  Response SLA: SOC lead engaged within 1 hour.
  Notification: CISO, relevant business unit VP; OSFI notification evaluated.

  P3 — MEDIUM
  Definition: Malware detection contained to a single endpoint; policy violation
              with no data loss; failed intrusion attempt with indicators of
              compromise (IOCs) identified.
  Response SLA: SOC analyst assigned within 4 hours.
  Notification: Information Security Manager; business unit ISSO.

  P4 — LOW
  Definition: Single failed phishing attempt with no credential compromise;
              anomalous access detected and explained; minor policy violation.
  Response SLA: Logged and reviewed within 2 business days.
  Notification: Recorded in SIEM; no immediate escalation required.

================================================================================
4. INCIDENT RESPONSE PHASES
================================================================================

4.1 DETECTION AND REPORTING

Any employee who observes unusual system behaviour, receives a suspicious
communication, or suspects a security incident must:

  1. Do NOT attempt to investigate or remediate the incident independently.
  2. Preserve evidence — do not power off affected devices unless instructed.
  3. Report immediately via one of:
     - SOC Hotline:       1-800-BMO-SOCC (24x7)
     - Email:             soc@bmo.com (monitored 24x7; PGP key available)
     - ServiceNow Portal: https://bmo.service-now.com > Security Incident
     - Slack:             #security-incidents (monitored by SOC)

4.2 TRIAGE AND CLASSIFICATION

Upon receiving a report, the SOC Tier 1 analyst will:

  - Assign a case number and record in the SIEM (Splunk)
  - Classify incident severity (P1–P4) within 30 minutes
  - Engage the Incident Commander for P1/P2 incidents
  - Begin initial log collection and IOC extraction

4.3 CONTAINMENT

Containment actions depend on severity and system type. Approved containment
actions include:

  Network isolation:
    - Block source IPs at perimeter firewall (Palo Alto PAN-OS)
    - Isolate affected VLAN segments via Cisco ISE quarantine policy
    - Revoke BGP peering for compromised routing infrastructure

  Endpoint containment:
    - Isolate endpoint via CrowdStrike Falcon (Real Time Response)
    - Disable Active Directory account of compromised user
    - Revoke OAuth tokens and invalidate active sessions in Azure AD

  Cloud containment:
    - Revoke IAM roles and access keys in Azure / AWS
    - Enable Storage Account firewall to restrict access
    - Snapshot affected VMs before any remediation action

  IMPORTANT: Containment actions on production Tier 1 systems (Core Banking,
  Payments, Trading) require approval from the Incident Commander AND the
  relevant Business Technology Senior VP before execution.

4.4 ERADICATION

After containment is confirmed, the SOC and Infrastructure teams will:

  - Remove malware, backdoors, and attacker persistence mechanisms
  - Patch or re-image affected systems using verified clean images
  - Reset all credentials that were exposed or potentially compromised
  - Rotate secrets, API keys, and certificates associated with affected services
  - Validate IOC removal using endpoint detection tools

4.5 RECOVERY

Systems are returned to production only after:

  - Eradication has been independently verified by a second SOC analyst
  - Vulnerability that allowed initial compromise has been patched/mitigated
  - Enhanced monitoring has been activated for affected systems (minimum 30 days)
  - Business owner has signed off on recovery readiness checklist

4.6 POST-INCIDENT REVIEW

A Post-Incident Review (PIR) is mandatory for all P1 and P2 incidents:

  - PIR must be completed within 5 business days of incident closure
  - PIR report covers: timeline, root cause, detection gaps, response gaps,
    corrective actions with owners and due dates
  - P1 PIR reports are reviewed by the Board Risk Committee
  - Lessons learned are incorporated into the Threat Intelligence feed and
    tabletop exercise programme

================================================================================
5. EVIDENCE PRESERVATION
================================================================================

Digital forensic evidence must be preserved in accordance with the following
chain-of-custody requirements:

  - Acquire forensic disk images before any remediation on affected systems
  - Use write-blocking hardware for physical media acquisition
  - Store forensic images in the BMO Evidence Vault (access: forensics@bmo.com)
  - Evidence retention: 5 years, or duration of litigation hold if applicable
  - All evidence handling must be documented in the incident case record

Do not perform live remediation on a system before a forensic image has been
captured, unless delay would cause additional data loss or system damage.

================================================================================
6. REGULATORY AND LEGAL NOTIFICATION
================================================================================

6.1 OSFI NOTIFICATION

Under OSFI Technology and Cyber Risk Guideline (October 2023), BMO must notify
OSFI within 24 hours of becoming aware of a technology or cyber incident that:

  - Has caused or has the potential to cause material impact to BMO's operations
  - Involves unauthorised access to or exfiltration of customer data
  - Affects BMO's ability to meet its financial obligations

The CISO is the designated OSFI notification contact. Notify via:
  - OSFI Incident Reporting Portal: https://osfi-bsif.gc.ca/incident
  - OSFI Emergency Line: (613) 990-7788

6.2 PIPEDA / PRIVACY BREACH NOTIFICATION

Where an incident involves a breach of personal information that poses a real
risk of significant harm, the Privacy Officer must be engaged immediately.
Notification to the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (OPC) is
required as soon as feasible.

Affected individuals must be notified directly if it is reasonable to believe
the breach creates a real risk of significant harm.

6.3 PAYMENT CARD INDUSTRY (PCI-DSS)

Incidents involving cardholder data environments must follow PCI-DSS Incident
Response procedures. Engage the PCI QSA team within 1 hour of classification.

================================================================================
7. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
================================================================================

  CISO:               Ultimate accountability for incident response programme;
                      approves P1 public communications; regulatory notifications.

  Incident Commander: Named on-call SOC lead; coordinates all response activities;
                      escalation authority for containment decisions.

  SOC Analysts:       24x7 detection, triage, containment, and evidence collection.

  Infrastructure:     Executes network isolation and system recovery actions.

  Legal & Compliance: Advises on regulatory notifications; manages litigation holds.

  Communications:     Manages internal and external communications for P1 incidents;
                      coordinates with Media Relations for public statements.

  All Employees:      Report suspected incidents; preserve evidence; do not discuss
                      incident details outside of the response team.

================================================================================
8. TRAINING AND TESTING
================================================================================

  - All SOC staff complete incident response training annually
  - Tabletop exercises conducted quarterly (at minimum one P1-scenario per year)
  - Penetration testing and red team exercises conducted annually
  - Phishing simulation programme run monthly for all BMO staff
